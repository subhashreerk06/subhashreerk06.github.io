About

Probing matter
at its most fundamental.

My fascination with physics began with a simple question: what is the universe actually made of, and why does mathematics describe it so perfectly? That question has taken me from Bengaluru to Glasgow, from undergraduate experiments to the frontiers of Quantum Chromodynamics.

I am drawn to the places where pure mathematics meets physical reality — where symmetry groups dictate particle interactions, where spacetime curvature encodes gravity, and where a computational lattice can reveal the behaviour of quarks and gluons that no experiment can directly observe.

Outside the lab, I believe deeply in teaching as a form of thinking. My time as a Teaching Assistant at the International Centre for Theoretical Sciences sharpened both my understanding and my ability to make complex ideas genuinely accessible.
